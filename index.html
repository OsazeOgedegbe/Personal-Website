About Me

I’m someone who genuinely enjoys solving problems, especially the kind people and businesses don’t even know they have.

While I was in school, I explored so many fields including Python programming, networking and web development. I thought I was going to end up as a full-stack web developer so I went deep into it and built multiple web apps using Django (Python-based framework).

Towards the end of my third year, a series of consecutive events led me directly into the field of data which made me focus less on web development and more on data analytics with a touch of data science. This was when I learnt how to analyze data and build somewhat complex Machine Learning models using various libraries and frameworks.

I finished school with this momentum and started my career in data analytics which was really exciting. I worked my way up from an intern to a full-time data analyst at a consulting company where I use tools like Excel, Python, Power Query and Power BI.

Over time, I realized I didn’t just want to analyze problems, I wanted to fix them too and the came at the same time that I found the Microsoft Power Up Program. After I took the course and learnt all about the Microsoft Power Platform, I decided to keep learning more and implementing my knowledge at the consulting firm where I work.

Since then, I’ve been building business apps, automating processes and connecting systems to help my team work smarter, not harder. I’ve worked with the entire Power Platform stack to improve operations and remove bottlenecks and I've loved every bit of it.

Now I’m diving deeper into Dynamics 365, learning how to build and setup full business systems (CRMs and ERPs) that solve real-world challenges. I’m on a journey to become a full-stack Power Platform & Dynamics 365 Consultant, handling both the business and technical sides of implementation.

If you're looking for someone who loves challenges and solving problems with technology, that’s me.
